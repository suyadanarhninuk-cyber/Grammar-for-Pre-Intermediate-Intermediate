Mixed Tenses Challenge

15 questions testing ALL tenses from Units 1–4. Can you get them all right?
0 / 15
💡 Each question could require ANY tense from Units 1–4: Present Simple, Present Continuous, Past Simple, Past Continuous, Present Perfect Simple, Present Perfect Continuous, Past Perfect Simple, Past Perfect Continuous, used to, or would. Read the context carefully!
Question 1
Water ______ at 100°C. (a scientific fact — always true)
Present Simple General facts and scientific truths → present simple. This is always true, not just happening now.
Question 2
Look! The prices ______ again! (a trend that is changing right now)
Present Continuous A changing trend happening now → present continuous. "Look!" signals something we can observe at this moment.
Question 3
She ______ to Paris three times last year. (specific finished time: last year)
Past Simple "Last year" = specific finished time → past simple. You cannot use present perfect with "last year."
Question 4
I ______ my homework when the phone rang. (background activity interrupted by a shorter action)
Past Continuous Background activity in progress → past continuous. The phone ringing (past simple) interrupts the background scene.
Question 5
______ to Japan? (asking about experience at any time in your life)
Present Perfect Life experience up to now, no specific time stated → present perfect + "ever". Past perfect would only work if asking about experience before another past event.
Question 6
I ______ this answer. Can you explain it to me? (a state verb — understanding)
Present Simple "Understand" is a state verb → always present simple. State verbs are never used in the continuous form.
Question 7
She ______ here since 2018. (started in the past and continues now)
Present Perfect "Since 2018" = started in the past and continues to now → present perfect (simple or continuous). Past simple would mean she no longer works here.
Question 8
He ______ a bicycle before he moved to the city. (a past state — something he no longer has)
Used to "Own" is a state verb → used to (NOT would). "Would" only works for past repeated actions, never states like own, be, know, live.
Question 9
By the time we arrived, the film ______. (one event before another in the past)
Past Perfect "By the time" + past simple → the earlier event uses past perfect. The film started BEFORE we arrived. "Already" goes between had and the main verb.
Question 10
Sorry I'm late — I ______ all morning. That's why I look so tired! (focus on the activity and its duration — explains the current state)
Present Perfect Continuous Focus on the activity and duration leading up to now, explaining a present result (looking tired) → present perfect continuous.
Question 11
When I was a child, we ______ football in the park every weekend. (a repeated past action/habit)
Would A repeated past action (not a state) → would + verb is perfect. "Used to play" would also be correct. "Every weekend" confirms this was habitual.
Question 12
I ______ three cups of coffee today. (counting completed actions in an unfinished time period)
Present Perfect Simple "Today" = unfinished time → present perfect. Counting (three cups) → simple, not continuous. Continuous emphasises duration, not quantity.
Question 13
The family ______ for months before they finally found a new home. (how long an activity continued before a past event)
Past Perfect Continuous Duration of an activity ("for months") leading up to another past event → past perfect continuous. This emphasises how long the search continued before the result.
Question 14
I ______ him since we were children. (a state verb — knowing someone — from past to now)
Present Perfect Simple "Since" = past to now → present perfect. "Know" is a state verb → never continuous. "I've known" is the only correct option.
Question 15
It's the best book I ______. (superlative + experience up to now)
Present Perfect "It's the best/first/longest… I've ever…" is a fixed structure using present perfect. It connects a superlative to your experience up to NOW.
