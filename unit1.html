Grammar Focus Task

Choose the correct verb form in each sentence.

Question 1
I ______ to do some sports activities.
"Want" is a state verb (expressing feeling/desire) and is not normally used in the continuous form. State verbs describe states, not actions — so we use the present simple.
Question 2
Our tennis team ______ for new people.
The present continuous + "always" emphasises an ongoing, continuous process or repeated situation. It often conveys that the action is characteristic or notable — here, the team is constantly seeking new members.
Question 3
______ pay to use the pools?
"Often" signals a regular habit or general frequency, which requires the present simple. "Have to" here expresses necessity as a general fact, not a temporary situation.
Question 4
We ______ anyone to book the swimming lanes or the gym equipment.
"Normally" shows this is a routine or general rule. General rules and policies take the present simple — not the continuous. Think of it as: this is always the case, not just right now.
Question 5
What time ______ you?
"Suit" describes a factual state or description — it's a state verb here (similar to "fit" or "match"). State verbs take the present simple, not the continuous.
Question 6
Great, well, I ______ that's everything.
When "think" expresses an opinion or belief, it acts as a state verb → present simple. You only use the continuous form ("I'm thinking about…") when describing the temporary action of pondering or considering something right now.

"We 1 use energy for three main things: electricity production, heating and transport. For the first two, we 2 ______ options such as solar and wind power, or natural gas. But oil is still the world's number one source of energy, and for transport at least, there is currently no alternative. In China, domestic energy consumption 3 ______ year by year and demand in similar regions 4 ______ fast. We 5 ______ how to use energy more efficiently now than in the past but the worldwide rise in demand 6 ______ current supply to expand. Some experts 7 ______ that we need to find a new source of energy soon."
Gap 2 — verb: have
"Have" here means possession (we possess options) — it's a state verb, so it takes the present simple. We don't say "we are having options."
Gap 3 — verb: go up
"Is going up" — the phrase "year by year" signals a changing, developing trend happening right now and continuing. This requires the present continuous, which we use for situations that are currently changing or in progress.
Gap 4 — verb: catch up
"Is catching up" — demand is currently in the process of catching up, and "fast" emphasises this is an ongoing, developing trend. Present continuous is used for changes happening now.
Gap 5 — verb: know
"Know" is a state verb describing thought and knowledge — it's never used in the continuous form in standard English. We say "we know" (present simple), not "we are knowing."
Gap 6 — verb: cause
"Is causing" — the worldwide rise in demand is currently and actively causing supply to expand. This is a situation that is changing right now, so we use the present continuous.
Gap 7 — verb: agree (or think)
Both "agree" and "think" are acceptable here. Both are state verbs expressing an opinion or shared belief — so they take the present simple. "Some experts agree / think that…" states a general position, not a temporary action.
