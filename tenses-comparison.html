All Tenses at a Glance

A complete comparison of every tense covered in Units 1–4. Use this as a study reference.

Present Tenses (Unit 1)

Present Simple

Unit 1
+   I/you/we/they work  |  he/she/it works
−   don't/doesn't + verb  |  ?   Do/Does … + verb?
  • Facts and general truths: Water boils at 100°C.
  • Habits and routines: I usually take the bus to work.
  • Permanent situations: She lives in London.
  • State verbs: I want, I know, I believe, I like, I own
alwaysusuallyoftensometimesneverevery daynormally

Present Continuous

Unit 1
+   am/is/are + verb-ing
−   am/is/are not + verb-ing  |  ?   Am/Is/Are … + verb-ing?
  • Actions happening NOW: She's studying in the library right now.
  • Temporary situations: I'm living with my parents at the moment.
  • Changing/developing trends: Prices are going up year by year.
  • always + continuous = emphasis/annoyance: He's always losing his keys!
nowright nowat the momentcurrentlythis weekthese days
⚠ State verbs (want, know, believe, like, own, seem, belong) are NOT normally used in the continuous form.
Past Tenses (Unit 2)

Past Simple

Unit 2
+   verb-ed / irregular past form
−   didn't + verb  |  ?   Did … + verb?
  • Completed actions with a finished time: I visited Paris last year.
  • A series of completed actions: She woke up, got dressed, and left.
  • Past states with specific duration: He worked there for 17 years.
yesterdaylast weekin 2010agowhen I was young

Past Continuous

Unit 2
+   was/were + verb-ing
−   was/were not + verb-ing  |  ?   Was/Were … + verb-ing?
  • Background scene for a past event: I was walking home when it started to rain.
  • Two actions in progress at the same time: While I was cooking, he was cleaning.
  • Temporary past situation: At that time, she was living in Spain.
whilewhenasat that timeall morning

used to & would

Unit 2
+   used to + verb  |  would + verb
−   didn't use to + verb  |  ?   Did … use to + verb?
  • used to — past states AND past habits: I used to live in Paris. / I used to walk to school.
  • would — past habits ONLY (not states): We would play football every Sunday.
⚠ would CANNOT be used with state verbs (own, be, live, know, like). Use used to instead.
⚠ Neither can be used with a specific duration: "He worked there for 17 years." (NOT used to work for 17 years)
Present Perfect Tenses (Unit 3)

Present Perfect Simple

Unit 3
+   have/has + past participle
−   haven't/hasn't + pp  |  ?   Have/Has … + pp?
  • Unfinished time period: I've written a plan this morning. (still morning)
  • Experience at an unstated time: Have you ever been to Buenos Aires?
  • Past action with present result: I've collected plenty of information. (I have it now)
  • Situation from past to now (for/since): I've worked here for three years.
  • How many times: I've read three articles this week.
everneveralreadyyetjustrecentlyso farforsincethis week
⚠ If you state WHEN something happened, use past simple: "I wasted time last week." (NOT have wasted)

Present Perfect Continuous

Unit 3
+   have/has been + verb-ing
−   haven't/hasn't been + verb-ing  |  ?   Have/Has … been + verb-ing?
  • How long (emphasis on duration): I've been reading for the past two weeks.
  • Focus on the activity itself (may be unfinished): I've been writing my essay. (maybe not done yet)
  • Explains a visible result: I've been running — that's why I'm sweating.
⚠ State verbs do NOT have a continuous form: "I've known them since childhood." (NOT been knowing)
Past Perfect Tenses (Unit 4)

Past Perfect Simple

Unit 4
+   had + past participle
−   hadn't + pp  |  ?   Had … + pp?
  • An earlier event before another past event: When I arrived, she had already left.
  • With when, by the time, after, as soon as: By the time he was six, he had written a composition.
  • Experience before a past point (ever/never/before): They were richer than they had ever been.
  • Unfulfilled hopes: She had expected to win, but she lost.
by the timewhenafterbeforealreadyjustneverever
⚠ If events are in chronological order, use past simple for both: "She woke up and left." (NOT had woken up and left)

Past Perfect Continuous

Unit 4
+   had been + verb-ing
−   hadn't been + verb-ing  |  ?   Had … been + verb-ing?
  • How long before a past point (duration): The family had been struggling for some time.
  • Focus on the activity leading up to a past moment: She had been making excellent progress.
⚠ Cannot use for counting: "I had visited her several times." (NOT had been visiting — use simple for how many)
⚠ State verbs → always simple: "I had known her for years." (NOT had been knowing)
Key Comparisons

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present Perfect ✔

I've been to Paris. (at some point — no specific time)

I've written 3 articles this week. (week not finished)

She's worked here since 2015. (still works here)

Past Simple ✔

I went to Paris last summer. (specific time stated)

I wrote 3 articles last week. (week is finished)

She worked there for 5 years. (no longer works there)

Simple vs Continuous (any tense)

Simple → result / how many / completion

I've written my essay. (it's finished)

I've read 3 books. (counting)

He had visited her twice. (how many)

Continuous → how long / activity / in progress

I've been writing my essay. (maybe not finished)

I've been reading all day. (duration)

They had been struggling for months. (how long)

used to vs would vs Past Simple

used to

Past states + habits

I used to own a car.

I used to walk to school.

would

Past habits ONLY

We would play every Sunday.

⚠ NOT states

Past Simple

Specific duration

He worked there for 17 years.

⚠ NOT used to/would

State Verbs — Never Continuous

WRONG ✘

I'm wanting to go.

She's knowing the answer.

I've been knowing him for years.

He had been owning a car.

CORRECT ✔

I want to go.

She knows the answer.

I've known him for years.

He had owned a car.

Common state verbs: want, need, know, believe, understand, like, love, hate, prefer, own, belong, seem, appear, mean, suppose, agree, disagree, fit, suit, contain, consist
