Grammar Focus Task

Read the sentences from the Portia spiders text. Match each one (1–3) to the correct grammatical explanation (a–c).

This exercise is based on a reading passage about Portia spiders — jumping spiders that hunt other spiders using remarkably intelligent strategies.
Sentence 1
The researchers allowed various prey spiders to spin webs in the laboratory and then introduced Portia spiders.
Correct answer: (b) a series of single past completed actions. Both "allowed" and "introduced" are in the Past Simple — they describe two actions that happened one after the other as completed events: first the researchers allowed, then they introduced.
Sentence 2
Portia spiders moved more when the webs were shaking than when they were still.
Correct answer: (a) a background scene and an action. "Were shaking" is the Past Continuous — it sets the background scene (the webs were already shaking). "Moved" is the Past Simple — the main action happening within that background.
Sentence 3
They noticed that the spiders would sometimes shake their quarry's web violently.
Correct answer: (c) a repeated action in the past. "Would sometimes shake" uses would + verb to describe a habitual or repeated action in the past. The word "sometimes" reinforces that this happened more than once.

Past Tense Rules

Choose the correct verb form for each sentence based on the rules for past tenses, states, and habits.

Question 1
He ______ a car before he moved to the city.
"Own" is a state verb (describing possession, not an action). While both used to and would can describe past repeated actions, you cannot use would for past states. Only used to works with state verbs like own, be, have, know, like, live.
Question 2
Bill Murphy ______ for the police force for 17 years.
You cannot use used to or would when you state exactly how long a past action lasted (e.g., "for 17 years"). When a specific duration is given, you must use the Past Simple. Used to implies a general past habit without a stated time frame.
Question 3
A woman ______ the police to report a robbery at her house.
This describes a single, completed action in the past — the woman made one phone call. Single completed past actions require the Past Simple. The Past Continuous ("was calling") would imply the action was in progress when something else happened — but no interrupting event is mentioned here.
Question 4
He was doing his homework in his bedroom when the burglar ______ into the house.
The Past Continuous ("was doing") sets the background scene — this was already happening. The Past Simple ("came") introduces the shorter action that interrupts the background. This is the classic pattern: Past Continuous (background) + Past Simple (interruption).
